Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is someones ability to recognize, understand, and manage both our own emotions and those of others as we are interacting with them.
It is important for social interactions, decision making and taking on challenges in a productive healthy way.
The main skills in emotional intelligence are:

  • The abilities to understand what makes yourself tick, how to get the best out of yourself by managing the types of situations you find challenging.
  • The ability to understand others through empathy so you can interact with others in a positive manner that allows for constructive outcomes.
  • The ability to clearly communicate with others to ensure interactions work towards a positive outcome.

How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ focuses on reasoning, problem-solving, logical thinking and the ability to understand abstract concepts.
The main skills in IQ are things like memory, mathematical ability and analytical thinking.
A high IQ is generally associated with ability to learn information, process it, and apply it to solving problems.

Why is EQ important?

Developing your EQ skills lets you interact with others in a more contructive and positive manner. As an employee, a supervisor/manager, it doesn't matter. It improves your ability to walk away from interactions with a positive outcome by having clear constructive communication that allows for everyone to understand eachother without conflict and misunderstandings.
EQ also helps when your not working with others by similarly understanding yourself to achieve better outcomes without getting as frustrated or blocked as you might otherwise.
