Values, Empathy and Self-Awareness in programming

Values are very important as you will more often than not be working in a team. Honesty, integrity, responsibility, following best practices, being professional and accountable are key to functioning as an effective team to get projects done on time and with as few hiccups as possible.

Empathy is important all the way through the programming process. From initially trying to put yourself in the users shoes to work out what exactly the user needs, wants, how it will be experienced. Through the development process where you need to always consider the users experience, how user friendly is it, how friendly for visually-impaired is it, etc. Then also being open to user feedback to connect with them where you may have room to improve design.
Empaty is also key for the collaborative side of programming. You will very often be on a team. Respecting others opinions, being understanding when things happen that might interfere with another team members performance or ability to get something done by a deadline. If the team is functioning well together, the whole process will be much more effective, efficient, and enjoyable.

Self-awareness is helpful for understanding your strengths and weakness. This allows for good decisions around where to best be able to benefit a team and where to personally develop so you are able to tackle all aspects of the job effectively.
Being self-aware allows for more growth opportunities whether through being open to constructive criticism or being able to seek out feedback and insight from more experienced programmers in areas you may be weaker in. Knowning your strengths, weaknesses and personal interests helps with applying to different companies where your strengths can be utilised and perhaps your personal interests align with what the company is doing.
Self-awareness is also key to managing your time effectively, knowning when you need a break, when you might need to implement different parts of your learning plan or well-being plan to ensure a healthy and productive state for you and your team.

Most Surprising Thing About Core Skills

For me its definitely how much I've actually enjoyed thinking about it and trying to implement changes in my life. Initially it kind of felt a bit "silly" or not overly useful but my attitude couldn't have been more wrong.
I've found I'm using these concepts core skills covered all the time. When things don't quite feel right I'm immediately considering:

  • My mindset, am I open to growth or stuck in a fixed mindset?
  • How I'm doing, when was my last break, do I need fresh eyes?
  • Have I been keeping up with my well-being plan?
I'm also really enjoying mindful listening in all my conversations. While you generally can't sit there and listen for 2 minutes straight without responding I have found it very satisfying and have enjoyed conversations more when I'm actually paying attention and processing what the other person is saying. My responses are better, they feel heard and are more engaged, all in all a great core skill for life.

Why focus on Core Skills?

Programming as an industry is so collaborative. You have to be able to healthily assess yourself and function in a team environment to be successful, keep yourself in a healthy productive mental and physical state rather than burning out or working on too little sleep/exercise/nutrition.
You can be incredible at the code side of things, but if you cant collaborate then its got very limited usefulness to an employer. Most projects will not be solo, most of them will require that you have some back and forward on ideas with a team to ensure the best product is developed. Understanding yourself, how to work with others, how to get the best out of others, how to receive and give effective feedback, etc. all come into play with function efficiently on a team.
